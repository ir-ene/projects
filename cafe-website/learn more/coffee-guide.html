Coffee Guide

  • Make sure that all your coffee tools are clean and dried. Leftover coffee grounds or build-up of coffee oil can make future cups of coffee taste bitter and rancid.
  • The quality of coffee is greatly determined by the quality of beans. Different factors include:
    • The country where the coffee is grown
    • The variety of bean
    • The roast type (dark, light etc.)
    • Texture of your ground
  • Fresh-roasted beans is important for a good tasting cup of coffee
  • Use filter or bottled water if your tap water does not taste good.
  • The 'golden ratio' for coffee is one to two tablespoons of ground coffee for every six ounces of water.
  • The optimal temperature for brewing coffee is between 195 to 205 degrees Fahrenheit. Colder water will result in flat coffee while water that is too hot will cause a loss of quality.
